P.62 Their intellectual horizon was therefore largely formed from headlines in the Guardian or the New York Times, which happened to be the same newspapers their Greek, Dutch and Belgian friends read. In their world, Barack Obama’s speeches and high school shootings existed far more vividly than the laws passed just a few U-Hahn stations away, or the refugees drowning two hours’ flight south.

P.110 But then, had they really chosen it? The reason they had tolerated, even loved the work, they will tell themselves, is because the repetitiveness provided a counterbalance tot the limitless growth and broad horizons of the rest of their days. Now, they will realise, nothing remains but the work.
